Word Classes

Noun:

A word used to identify people, places, or things.
  Example: “book”

Plural Noun:

A word used to identify multiple people, places, or things.
  Example: “chairs”

Verb:

A word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence.
  Example: “jump”

Adverb:

A word used to modify or qualify an adjective, verb, or another adverb (usually ending in “-ly”).
  Example: “quickly”

Adjective:

A word naming an attribute, added to a noun to modify or describe it.
  Example: “blue”

Name:

A word used to identify a person.
  Example: “Ryan”

Animal:

A living organism other than a human.
  Example: “dog”

Number:

A word, symbol, or figure describing an arithmetical value that represents a particular quantity. Used in counting, making calculations, and for showing order in a series or for identification.
  Example: “13” or “thirteen”

Country:

The name of a nation with its own government, occupying a particular territory.
  Example: “Canada”

Body Part:

A word describing any part of a person’s body.
  Example: “ear”
